01 — Brand Overview

The Story

Monster Mash-Up is a JRDA regulation youth roller derby tournament in Sacramento, CA — October 24–25, 2026. Built for fearless skaters and the communities that cheer them on. Every element of this brand is designed to feel like the best Halloween of your life, on wheels.

Campy
We lean into theatrical drama. Bigger, bolder, more monstrous.
Fierce
These are real athletes. The brand respects the sport and the skaters.
Playful
Halloween puns always welcome. Fun is the whole point.
Bold
Big type. Strong color. Nothing timid or apologetic.
Inclusive
Youth-forward and community-driven. Everyone belongs here.
Vintage
Creepshow inspired and vintage pop art energy. Handcrafted and alive.
This brand is
  • Fun, theatrical, and over the top
  • Spooky in a campy, vintage Creepshow inspired way
  • Fierce and empowering for youth athletes
  • Community-driven and welcoming to families
  • Warm without being soft
This brand is not
  • Gory, genuinely scary, or horror-adjacent
  • Corporate, sterile, or overly polished
  • Overly cute or childish
  • Exclusionary or intimidating
  • Generic Halloween clipart energy
02 — Logo

Logo Usage

The Monster Mash-Up wordmark uses CCMeltdown — a drip-horror display font exclusive to the logo. Do not use CCMeltdown anywhere else in the brand system. Its restraint is what makes it special.

Acid Monster
Creature Lagoon

Creature Lagoon

Logo do's
  • Use approved color versions only
  • Maintain clear space — minimum height of the letter M on all sides
  • Scale proportionally, never stretch or squish
  • Use vector files for all print production
  • Minimum 1 inch in print · 80px digital
Logo don'ts
  • Never use CCMeltdown anywhere except the logo
  • Never recolor outside the approved palette
  • Never add drop shadows, outlines, or effects
  • Never place on busy backgrounds without a solid container
  • Never rotate or apply any transformation

Color rules
  • Never use teal and Acid Monster at the same size or in adjacent roles — one leads, one supports
  • Never use pure white (#ffffff) as body text on dark backgrounds — always use bone white (#ffe9c3)

  • Mustard and tan are texture/supporting colors only — never use as primary accent

05 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Campy and theatrical. Encouraging and fierce. Punny and playful. The tone shifts by context — but the personality never changes.

Tone by context
ContextTone
Social mediaPlayful, punny, high energy — Halloween puns always on the table
Skater recruitmentFierce and empowering — speak to the athlete, not the child
Parent-facingWarm, clear, and trustworthy — all details needed, no jargon
Referee / officialCollegial and humorous — the ref community gets the joke, lean in
Sponsor materialsProfessional and enthusiastic — credible and community-focused
Day-of announcementsMaximum hype and celebration — no restraint
On-brand vocabulary
HauntSlayCreepGhoulMonsterDareUnleash Speed demonRollFearlessFierceMash-up
Avoid

Anything genuinely gory or frightening. Corporate or exclusionary language. Overly childish or condescending tone. Generic Halloween phrases with no personality.

TikTok content series
Series 1 — Are You Monster Enough?
Skater recruitment content. POV skating clips, slow-mo hits, monster makeup transitions. "Skate if you dare" as recurring hook. Ends each video with sign-up Call to Action.
Series 2 — Ref Wanted: Dead or Alive
Funny recruitment content for referees and volunteers. "We need brave souls" — "no experience with the undead required." Campy horror angle, collegial humor.
Series 3 — Countdown to Chaos
Weekly countdown as event approaches. Behind the scenes, venue setup, team reveals, bracket drops. Build tension like a horror movie trailer.
Series 4 — Get Your Tickets Before We Haunt You
Ticket sales push with urgency and humor. Skaters "haunt" people who didn't buy tickets yet. Last-chance energy with genuine comedy.
Social rules
  • Always use bone white (#ffe9c3) for text — never pure white on dark backgrounds
  • Never post unedited photos — apply brand color grading and filter treatment
  • Always include at least #MonsterMashUp in every post
  • Never use off-palette colors — if it's not in the brand guide, it doesn't go in the post
  • Always design mobile-first — every post must be legible on a 375px phone screen
  • Never use teal and Acid Monster at the same size in the same post
